Why I built Annam.

A note from the founder

It was 2023. I was 23, living in a paying-guest accommodation in Bengaluru, working a nightshift in an operational role.

The PG provided food. But the quality was bad. Really bad — oily, repetitive, served at the wrong temperatures, sometimes from food that had been sitting around for too long. You can't expect a healthy body when this is what you're eating every day. And given my lifestyle — sleep at sunrise, work through the night — it mattered more than ever.

Whenever I tried to take diet seriously — order something nutritious, find healthier options — the costs would shoot up. Eating well in a metro city, on a metro salary, while working irregular hours felt impossible. Homely food was missing from my life entirely. Months of daily outside food from random places eventually made me fall sick.

I wasn't able to find proper food. I started missing home — really missing it. And then I realised: there are millions of people like me. Students. Working professionals. Nightshift workers. People who moved to cities chasing opportunity, and lost their health in the process. We weren't bad at eating — we just didn't have the systems.

So I decided to build the answer I wish I'd had — an organisation that provides homely food in a healthy way, so the well-being of people like us is finally taken care of.

My partner is finishing her food-science programme, watching how diet platforms work everywhere from RX-grade clinical programs to commercial meal kits. We know Indian food, with its incredible regional diversity, deserves the same level of care. Annam means food, sustenance, life — in Tamil and Sanskrit, the same word. It's the most sacred form of life itself. That's what we want to deliver, every single day.

— Rishi
Founder, Annam · Bengaluru · 2026

Questions

Frequently asked questions.

Everything you might want to know before you start. Still unsure? book a ₹199 consult and ask your dietitian directly.

How does an Annam membership work?
You start with a free assessment, then a ₹199 video consultation. Your dietitian recommends a program (3, 6 or 12 months), explains everything, and only then — if you choose to continue — do you begin. You get a personalised plan plus weekly follow-ups and adjustments throughout.
Why don't you show prices on the website?
Nutrition isn't one-size-fits-all, so neither is the right program for you. Your dietitian shares clear, honest pricing during your consultation, based on your goals and the level of support you need — with no hidden charges and no obligation to continue.
What's included in the ₹199 consultation?
A 30-minute video call covering your goals, a review of your health assessment and any reports, your lifestyle, and a clear recommendation of the right program. The ₹199 is adjusted against any program you purchase within 7 days.
Do you use AI to create my diet plan?
No. Annam never uses AI to generate customer diet plans. Every plan is created, reviewed and approved by a qualified human dietitian (MSc / RD). We use technology only behind the scenes — to organise information, schedule follow-ups and keep your records secure.
Can Annam help with PCOS / PCOD?
Yes. Our dietitians build PCOS-focused plans around insulin sensitivity, balanced meals and sustainable habits — using regional Indian food you already eat. Bring any recent reports to your consultation so your dietitian can personalise accordingly. (This is nutrition guidance and supports, but does not replace, your doctor's care.)
Can Annam help with diabetes or pre-diabetes?
Yes. Plans can be tailored for blood-sugar management — meal timing, carbohydrate quality and portion structure — in coordination with your medical treatment. Please continue any medication and check-ups with your doctor; our dietitians complement that care, they don't replace it.
Is the food vegetarian, and is it regional?
Your plan is built around your preferences — vegetarian, non-vegetarian, eggetarian, Jain, and regional cuisines (North Indian, South Indian, Bengali, Gujarati and more). The whole point is to work with the food you already love, not against it.
Do I get recipes?
Yes. Your plan includes personalised recipes and food guidance so you know exactly what to cook and how. Meal delivery and chef-at-home are future services — for now, you cook, and your dietitian guides you.
How do the weekly follow-ups work?
Your dietitian checks your progress every week, answers questions over WhatsApp, and adjusts your plan as your body, schedule and goals change. You're never left to figure it out alone.
Can I pause my membership?
Yes. Travelling, on vacation, or just need a break? You can pause your membership and resume whenever you're ready — your plans, history and progress stay safe in My Diet.
Can I cancel, and what about refunds?
There's no obligation to continue after your consultation. The ₹199 consult is covered by our satisfaction policy, and your dietitian explains all terms clearly before you pay for any program. Longer programs are structured commitments, not silent auto-renewals.
Do I need lab reports to start?
No — they're optional but helpful. If you have recent reports (lipid profile, HbA1c, thyroid, vitamin D/B12, etc.), sharing them helps your dietitian personalise faster. If not, your dietitian will advise whether any tests are worth doing.
Do you deliver meals or send a chef?
Not yet. At launch, Annam offers personalised diet plans only. Meal delivery and chef-at-home are on our roadmap as future services — we'll launch them only once they meet our quality standards.
Who are the dietitians?
Qualified nutrition professionals holding MSc / RD credentials. You work with a real human dietitian throughout — during your consult, in your plan, and in your weekly follow-ups.
How is my health data handled?
Your information is private and used only to personalise your care. Health records, reports and plans are kept secure and visible only to you and your assigned dietitian. See our Privacy page for details.

Common questions

Things people ask us.

How is this different from a franchise?+

A franchise charges an upfront brand fee (₹15–50 lakh typically) plus ongoing royalty (5–15% of revenue) and binds you to strict operational rules. Our programme has zero brand fee, zero royalty — only a transparent revenue share on each order we send you. You keep your independence, your kitchen name, and can leave anytime with 30 days notice.

What's the revenue share?+

Annam takes 28–35% of each order's value, depending on cuisine complexity and city. The rest goes to you to cover ingredients, packaging, gas, electricity, and your earnings. We disclose the exact percentage during the discovery call. As you scale, the share trends to the lower end of that range — better margins for committed kitchens.

What if I don't have ₹2 lakh to invest?+

We help you apply for Mudra loans (up to ₹10 lakh, low interest) and Stand-Up India loans (specifically for SC/ST/women entrepreneurs, up to ₹1 crore). Most kitchen-partners use a mix of personal savings + Mudra. Our team walks you through the paperwork.

Why does management approval matter?+

One bad kitchen can ruin Annam's reputation for thousands of customers. Our quality team reviews every kitchen — hygiene, equipment, recipe execution, packaging — before activation. No kitchen receives orders until management approval is granted. If something doesn't meet standards, we tell you exactly what to fix and re-audit free of charge.

Can men apply?+

Absolutely yes — our programme is open to everyone. We highlight the women-entrepreneur angle because India's home cooks are predominantly women whose talent is structurally undervalued, and we want to fix that. But the application process and economics are identical regardless of gender.

What cities are you in?+

Currently active: Mumbai, Pune, Bangalore, Hyderabad. Launching in 2026: Chennai, Delhi NCR, Kolkata. If you're outside these cities, you can still apply — we'll add you to the waitlist and prioritise expansion based on demand.

How many hours a day do I need to work?+

Starter kitchens (30–60 meals/day): typically 4–5 hours, mostly during lunch and dinner prep windows. Pro kitchens (150+ meals/day): 7–9 hours with one or two helpers. You set your daily capacity in the vendor app — if you can only cook 30 meals on a given day, we won't send you 50.
